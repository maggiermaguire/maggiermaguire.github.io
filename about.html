Bio

I'm a product designer with a computer science background, which shapes how I approach ambiguous, technically complex problems. At Buildium, I've spent the last few years working across the full stack of a growing SaaS product — from redesigning core electronic payment workflows to architecting the company's first AI-native design patterns for trust, error-handling, and human-in-the-loop interaction.

Earlier in my career, I worked as a product designer on a cross-functional team at McKinsey & Company, transforming a complex Excel-based sustainability model into a fully tech-enabled solution, and before that spent time on the product management side at ServiceNow, leading the launch of an updated Customer Success Center. That mix of design, research, and product management experience is why I care as much about the systems and workflows behind a product as the interface itself.

Outside of work, I live in New York City. I'm a marathon runner chasing my second World Major in Berlin this fall, a devoted supporter of live theater working my way through a growing list of Broadway shows, and an avid traveler who's visited more than 27 countries. I'm also a huge appreciator of well-designed products that make everyday life easier.

Philosophy

Design in the system, not just the screen

A single screen rarely tells the whole story. I look for the patterns, tokens, and rules that keep a product consistent as it scales.

Make the ambiguous testable

Especially with AI-driven interfaces, the fastest way through uncertainty is a prototype you can put in front of real users.

Trust is a design problem

How a product communicates confidence, handles errors, and explains itself matters as much as how it looks.

Make the abstract concrete

Turning ambiguity into actionable strategy starts with going slow enough to find the right direction, then bringing that strategy to life by shipping to customers.

Creativity through constraints

Constraints aren't a barrier to design around — accepting them and designing within them is where the more interesting solutions live.

Focus on impact

Prioritizing the work that delivers the most value for customers, teams, and the business.

Stay in the details

Staying close enough to the work — technically and otherwise — to ask the right questions, solve the right problems, and earn trust.
